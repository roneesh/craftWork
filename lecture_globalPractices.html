Global Practices and Economic Sustainability: The Ethical Implications of Local and International Crafts Production

About This Lecture

As the market for handmade goods blossoms locally, nationally and internationally, how do we create successful craft-based businesses that are economically viable, environmentally sustainable, and that have fair labor practices? In this panel, Maureen Dunn of Mata Traders, Harish I Patel of Ishi Vests and Jen Beeman of Grainline studio discussed the realities of owning a crafts-based business locally, nationally and internationally. The panel was followed by a shibori dye workshop with Nora Renick Rinehart.

This lecture was held February 18th, 2014 at Beauty and Brawn Gallery in Logan Square, Chicago.
