Lecture - Identity in Craft

About This Lecture

Gender, class and race have had (and continue to have) a profound impact on how different crafts are categorized, valued, displayed and taught both inside and outside the art world. Whether it is the struggle for fiber arts to find a place within the mainstream art world, the attempt by the Arts and Crafts Movement to address the alienation of factory workers, or the current white, female and middle class representation of the "DIY Revolution", you can't talk about the intersection of art and craft without talking about issues of identity.

This panel featured artists Noelle Garcia, Jeff Rhodes, Carrie Ann Schumacher, and Jasmin Guerrero who discussed the intersection of race, class and gender on their work as artists and crafters.

The discussion was followed by a hands on demonstration of Native American bead weaving techniques, led by Noelle Garcia.
