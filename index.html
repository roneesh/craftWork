Craft/Work

Craft can be all sorts of things: it can be a verb (as in, "to craft"), and adjective ("it's crafty") and a noun ("to have a craft", i.e. a skill). It is an appropriate descriptor for such widely ranging arenas as self and family taught handicrafts to haute couture fashion production.

Craft/Work grew out of an ongoing conversation between Nora Renick-Rinehart and Rachel Wallis about the relationship between art and craft, both in our own work and in the world at large. Thanks to the support of our Kickstarter backers, in 2014 Craft/Work organized a series of artist talks and hands-on workshops exploring questions of labor, value, gender, race, technology and more in the creation and marketing of fine art and craft. The workshop series culminated in a gallery exhibit featuring the work of 13 diverse artists and collectives.
