Continuing Conversation: The Past, Present and Future of the Art vs. Craft Debate

Listen on Media Fire

About This Lecture

The definitions we use to distinguish art and craft are built upon centuries of cultural debate and technological innovations. In this presentation, Keeley Marie Stitt, Nora Renick Rinehart and Molly Roth Scranton took a closer look at a few specific moments in history when the conversation changed: the Renaissance, the Industrial Revolution, the 1960's-70's, and Modern/Contemporary Art.

The presentation was followed by a participatory workshop entitled Ways of Valuing with Keeley Marie Stitt that explores the concepts of Use Value, Exchange Value and Sign Value.

This lecture was held February 8th, 2014 at Beauty and Brawn Gallery in Logan Square, Chicago.
