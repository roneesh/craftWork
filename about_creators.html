About Craft/Work

The Inaugural Craft/Work gallery exhibit

Beauty and Brawn Art Gallery and Think Space Logan Square, Chicago.

April 12, 2014 to May 31, 2014

Craft can be all sorts of things: it can be a verb (as in, "to craft"), and adjective ("it's crafty") and a noun ("to have a craft", i.e. a skill).

It can describe such wide ranging arenas as self and family taught handicrafts to haute couture fashion production.

Craft/Work grew out of an ongoing conversation between Nora Renick-Rinehart and Rachel Wallis about the relationship between art and craft, both in our own work and in the world at large.

Thanks to the support of our Kickstarter backers, in 2014 Craft/Work organized a series of artist talks and hands-on workshops exploring questions of labor, value, gender, race, technology and more in the creation and marketing of fine art and craft. The workshop series culminated in a gallery exhibit featuring the work of 13 diverse artists and collectives.

Nora Renick Rinehart
Nora Renick Rinehart considers herself both an artist and a crafter. Her practice has included making pieces to show in galleries, selling wares online and at craft-fairs and simply making for the sheer joy of it. Her current passion resides in teaching all things textiles here in Chicago.

Rachel Wallis
Rachel Wallis is a crafter and self-taught artist. She's organized a number of small shows and art based fundraisers and is a contributing member of El Stitch y Bitch, a knitting and sewing group here in Chicago.

Roneesh Vashisht
Roneesh met Nora while learning to sew! And after deciding she was the best teacher in the universe, wanted to help her out with any and all web sites! In addition to that he's a total maker: engineering, programming, welding, woodworking, sewing, you name it he tries to do it.
